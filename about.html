About ReRadar

Real estate intelligence,
built for everyone

ReRadar exists because the data that serious real estate investors rely on has always sat behind expensive paywalls, obscure databases, or hours of manual research. We're changing that — one tool at a time, free.

What we're building

ReRadar is a real estate intelligence platform that pulls from public data sources — FRED, Zillow Research, Redfin Data Center, Census Bureau, SEC EDGAR, and institutional research — and surfaces it in tools that anyone can actually use.

Whether you're an investor analyzing your first rental property or a capital markets broker tracking CMBS maturities, the platform is designed to give you the same quality of data that institutional desks have — without the $2,000/month CoStar subscription.

Our data sources

All data on ReRadar comes from public sources. We don't sell data, don't fabricate numbers, and clearly label where every data point comes from and when it was last updated.

Commercial tools (Maturity Radar, Distress Monitor) currently use illustrative data modeled on public CMBS research from CRED iQ, Trepp, and Colliers. As we grow, we'll integrate direct data feeds. All illustrative data is clearly labeled.

Free, and why that matters

ReRadar is free to use because we believe the information asymmetry in real estate — where institutions have data and individual investors don't — is one of the most solvable problems in the market. You don't need a Wall Street subscription to understand if a deal makes sense.

We'll eventually introduce optional premium features for power users. The core tools will always be free.

Built with

ReRadar is a lightweight, fully static platform — no backend required, no user data collected, no tracking. Every tool runs entirely in your browser using public APIs and open data. The source data is attributed on every page.
